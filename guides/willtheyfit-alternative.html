A Free willtheyfit.com Alternative

If you've used willtheyfit.com to check a wheel and tire setup, this tool does the same core job — comparing two fitments side by side — for free, with no sign-up, and a cleaner live readout.

What it does

Enter your current wheels and the new ones, and Wheel & Tire Fitment Calculator shows, updating live as you type:

  • Poke & inset — how far the new wheel sits out past the hub face and in toward the strut
  • Offset (ET) comparison between the two setups
  • Rolling diameter & ride-height change from a taller or shorter tire
  • Speedometer error and your true speed at a given indicated speed
  • A cross-section diagram that overlays both wheels so you can see the difference

How it's different

  • Free and no sign-up. Nothing to install, no account, and nothing you enter leaves your browser.
  • Live, not "calculate." Every number and the diagram update as you drag a slider or type — no button to press.
  • Metric or imperial. Work in millimeters and km/h or inches and mph.
  • Built to be readable. A glossary and a labelled diagram explain what each number means and where it's measured.

An honest note on accuracy

Poke, inset, offset, ride-height and speedometer figures are calculated exactly. Overall rolling diameter uses the standard formula (rim + twice the sidewall) and does not model tire stretch, so that one figure can differ by a few millimeters from a heavily stretched setup — we flag it in the tool. As with any fitment tool, the output is an estimate: real-world clearance also depends on your suspension, camber, fender rolling and ride height.
